## Ingredients

### Seasoned Beef
- 2 lbs beef, cut into pieces
- Salt and pepper
- Onion powder
- Paprika
- 1 tsp carnitas taco seasoning
- 1 cup shiitake mushrooms, diced
- 2 cups spinach, chopped

### Roasted Base
- Potatoes (your choosing)
- Kabocha squash
- Salt and pepper
- Cheese (for topping potatoes)

### Cottage Cheese Sauce
- 1 cup cottage cheese
- 2 tbsp dill
- ¼ cup parsley
- Salt and pepper to taste
- Onion powder to taste
- Dash of paprika
- 1 clove garlic

### Yogurt BBQ Sauce
- Yogurt
- Barbecue sauce

### Fresh Toppings
- Spinach or lettuce
- Cucumbers

## Steps

### Roasted Base
1. Preheat oven to 350°F
2. Cut potatoes and season with salt and pepper
3. Roast potatoes for ~45 minutes
4. Cut kabocha squash and season with salt and pepper
5. Roast squash for ~25 minutes (until cooked but still firm)
6. Add cheese to potatoes while still hot

### Seasoned Beef
1. Put beef in a bowl
2. Season with salt, pepper, onion powder, paprika, and carnitas taco seasoning
3. Mix together
4. Heat a pan to medium-high
5. Brown the beef
6. When beef is about halfway browned, add diced mushrooms and chopped spinach

### Cottage Cheese Sauce
1. Blend cottage cheese, dill, parsley, garlic, salt, pepper, onion powder, and paprika in a blender until smooth

### Yogurt BBQ Sauce
1. Mix yogurt and barbecue sauce together

### Assembly
1. Layer roasted potatoes and kabocha squash as the base of the bowl
2. Top with seasoned beef, mushrooms, and spinach
3. Add fresh spinach or lettuce and cucumbers
4. Drizzle both sauces on top

## Notes

- This bowl follows burger-style flavor logic — future variations could include caramelized onions or other burger toppings
- The squash should stay firm, not mushy
- Both sauces get drizzled on top at the end
